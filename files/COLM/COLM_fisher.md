Here is a Philip A. Fisher analysis of Columbia Sportswear Company (COLM).

**Company, date of analysis, primary sources reviewed:** Columbia Sportswear Company, August 24, 2025, primary sources reviewed were the company's 2024 10-K filed on February 27, 2025 and earnings call transcripts from 2023 through August 2025.

**1) Products/Services with Long Runway**
**Question:** Does the company have products or services with sufficient market potential to make possible a sizable increase in sales for at least several years?
**Reasoning & Evidence:** Columbia Sportswear operates in the large and growing outdoor and active lifestyle apparel and footwear market. The company is executing a strategy to attract younger, more active consumers through its "ACCELERATE Growth Strategy," which aims to elevate the brand and drive growth. While U.S. sales have been challenged recently, the company is seeing strong international growth in its Europe, Middle East, and Africa (EMEA) and Latin America and Asia Pacific (LAAP) regions, indicating significant geographic expansion potential. The company also has a portfolio of brands (Columbia, SOREL, Mountain Hardwear, prAna) that address different consumer segments within the outdoor market.

**5) Worthwhile Profit Margins**
**Question:** Does the company have a worthwhile profit margin?
**Reasoning & Evidence:** In fiscal year 2024, Columbia Sportswear reported a gross profit margin of 50.2% and an operating margin of 8.0%. The gross margin expanded by 60 basis points from 49.6% in 2023, driven by favorable freight costs and sales mix, though this was partially offset by higher clearance and promotional activity. However, operating income and margin declined from 8.9% in the prior year due to increased SG&A expenses. While the gross margin is healthy, the operating margin is under pressure.

Synthesis:
Columbia Sportswear Company scores well on many of Fisher's points, particularly those related to long-term strategy, management stability and integrity, financial strength, and candor. The strongest positives are its debt-free balance sheet, commitment to long-range growth strategies over short-term profits, and a stable, experienced executive team. The most significant negatives are the current pressure on profit margins and the extremely high employee turnover in its retail and distribution operations, which raises questions about personnel relations. The variables most likely to change the score in the next 12-24 months are the success of the "ACCELERATE Growth Strategy" in reinvigorating U.S. sales and the effectiveness of the expanded "Profit Improvement Program" in boosting operating margins. A "No" on "Worthwhile Profit Margins" would flip to "Yes" with sustained improvement in operating margin back above 10%, while the "No" on "Labor & Personnel Relations" would require evidence of a significant and sustained reduction in employee turnover rates.
