### Network Effects
**Reasoning:** Oshkosh's business does not benefit from network effects. The value of its products does not increase for a customer as more customers use them. The company's products are self-contained and do not create a user network.
**Evidence:**
*   There is no evidence of network effects in the company's business model, as described in its public filings and transcripts.

### Switching Costs
**Reasoning:** Customers in the defense and fire & emergency segments face high switching costs due to the specialized nature of the products, long-term service and support contracts, and the need for extensive training. The vocational segment has lower, but still present, switching costs related to fleet commonality and operator familiarity.
**Evidence:**
*   Long-term contracts are common in the defense sector, creating a strong tie between Oshkosh and its customers. For example, the company has a multi-year contract with the U.S. Department of Defense for the Joint Light Tactical Vehicle (JLTV) program.
*   Oshkosh provides extensive aftermarket parts and services, which creates a long-term relationship with customers and increases the cost and complexity of switching to a competitor.
**Score:** 3 / 5.

**Evidence:**
*   As a large-scale manufacturer, Oshkosh has significant purchasing power, allowing it to procure raw materials and components at favorable prices.
*   The company's focus on operational excellence and continuous improvement initiatives helps to drive down production costs and improve efficiency.
**Score:** 3 / 5.

### Moat Duration Justification
Oshkosh's moat is primarily based on its strong position in the defense and fire & emergency markets, where it benefits from high barriers to entry, long-term customer relationships, and a reputation for quality and reliability. The company's moat is likely to be durable for at least 10-15 years, as it would be difficult for a new competitor to replicate its scale, distribution network, and regulatory expertise.

### Top 3 Moat Erosion Scenarios
1.  **A significant reduction in U.S. defense spending** could have a material impact on Oshkosh's financial performance, as the defense segment is a major contributor to the company's revenue and profitability.
2.  **The emergence of a new technology** that disrupts the traditional heavy-duty vehicle and equipment market could erode Oshkosh's competitive advantage. For example, the development of autonomous vehicles or electric-powered equipment could create opportunities for new competitors to enter the market.
3.  **A major operational failure or quality issue** could damage Oshkosh's reputation and lead to a loss of customers.
