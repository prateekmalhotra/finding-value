### 3) Near-Term Catalysts (0–12 months)

#### **1. Catalyst Title: Accelerated Share Repurchase Program Execution**

*   **Type:** Corporate Action
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** On March 19, 2025, the Board authorized an additional $53.4 million for share repurchases, bringing the total authorization to $100.0 million. As of February 28, 2025, $46.6 million was remaining under the previous authorization.
*   **Mechanism:** Aggressive share buybacks reduce the number of outstanding shares, which in turn increases earnings per share (EPS). A higher EPS, even with constant net income, can lead to a higher stock price, assuming a stable P/E multiple. It also signals management's confidence in the company's undervaluation.
*   **Timing:** August 2025 – August 2026
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** The company repurchases $50 million worth of shares. At an average price of $35/share, this would retire approximately 1.43 million shares.
    *   **Base Scenario:** The company repurchases $75 million worth of shares. At an average price of $35/share, this would retire approximately 2.14 million shares.
    *   **High Scenario:** The company fully executes the $100.0 million authorization. At an average price of $35/share, this would retire approximately 2.86 million shares.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** 10-20%. A significant reduction in share count can directly boost EPS, and signal strong capital return to shareholders, attracting investors and potentially re-rating the stock.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 90%. The company has a history of executing share repurchase programs, and the recent authorization signals a strong intention to continue.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** The company must have sufficient free cash flow to execute the repurchases without compromising operational needs or taking on excessive debt. Market conditions must be favorable for repurchases.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:**
    *   Quarterly reports on the amount of shares repurchased (Form 10-Q).
    *   Changes in cash and debt levels on the balance sheet.
    *   Announcements of any new repurchase authorizations.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:**
    *   A significant downturn in business performance could divert cash flow away from repurchases.
    *   The stock price could increase significantly, making repurchases less attractive.
    *   Management might decide to use the cash for other purposes, such as acquisitions or debt repayment.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch. Monitor the pace of share repurchases in the upcoming quarterly reports.

#### **2. Catalyst Title: New Major Book Releases Driving Stronger Than Expected Sales**

*   **Type:** Product
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** The company has recently released the 13th book in the popular *Dog Man* series and is set to release the 5th book in *The Hunger Games* series. These major releases are expected to be significant revenue drivers for the trade channel.
*   **Mechanism:** Strong sales from blockbuster book series can significantly boost revenues and profits in the Children's Book Publishing and Distribution segment. This can lead to upward revisions in earnings guidance and increased investor confidence, driving the stock price higher.
*   **Timing:** August 2025 – May 2026
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** The new releases meet modest expectations, contributing an additional $20 million in revenue.
    *   **Base Scenario:** The new releases perform well, in line with previous successful launches in their respective series, contributing an additional $50 million in revenue.
    *   **High Scenario:** The new releases become major bestsellers, exceeding expectations and contributing over $75 million in revenue, along with a halo effect on backlist sales.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** 5-15%. A strong performance from these key franchises would positively impact the company's largest segment, but the overall impact on the consolidated financials would be in the small to medium range.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 80%. These are established and popular series with a dedicated fanbase, making a certain level of success highly probable.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** The books must be well-received by their target audience. The company's marketing and distribution efforts must be effective.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:**
    *   Weekly and monthly book sales charts (e.g., NPD BookScan, Publisher's Weekly).
    *   Company's quarterly revenue figures for the Children's Book Publishing and Distribution segment.
    *   Management commentary on the performance of new releases in earnings calls.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:**
    *   The new books could underperform expectations.
    *   A general downturn in the retail book market could dampen sales.
    *   Competition from other major book releases or other forms of entertainment could draw away readers.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch. Track the initial sales data for the new releases and look for any mentions in the company's next earnings report.

### 4) Long-Term Catalysts (12–60 months)

#### **1. Catalyst Title: Successful Integration and Synergy Realization from 9 Story Acquisition**

*   **Type:** M&A / Operational
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** Scholastic acquired 9 Story Media Group, a leading independent creator, producer, and distributor of children's content, to enhance its entertainment and licensing business. The successful integration of this acquisition is a key long-term catalyst.
*   **Mechanism:** The 9 Story acquisition provides Scholastic with a robust platform to leverage its vast library of intellectual property (IP) across different media formats, including television and film. Synergies can be realized through cost savings, cross-promotion, and the creation of new revenue streams from licensing and merchandising.
*   **Timing:** August 2026 – August 2030
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** The integration process is slow, and only minor cost synergies of $5 million annually are achieved.
    *   **Base Scenario:** The company successfully integrates 9 Story, leading to a 10% increase in revenues for the Entertainment segment and $15 million in annual cost and revenue synergies.
    *   **High Scenario:** The integration is highly successful, creating a powerful entertainment engine that drives a 25% or more increase in Entertainment segment revenues and over $30 million in annual synergies.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** 15-25%. A successful integration can transform the Entertainment segment into a significant growth driver for the company, leading to a re-evaluation of the company's long-term growth prospects and a higher stock valuation.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 75%. While M&A integration always carries risks, the strategic fit between Scholastic's IP and 9 Story's production capabilities is strong, increasing the likelihood of success.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** A smooth integration of the two corporate cultures. The ability to successfully develop and market new content based on Scholastic's IP. The continued demand for high-quality children's content.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:**
    *   Revenue and operating income growth in the Entertainment segment.
    *   Announcements of new productions based on Scholastic IP.
    *   Licensing and merchandising revenue growth.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:**
    *   Cultural clashes and integration difficulties could lead to a loss of key talent and operational inefficiencies.
    *   The company could fail to successfully monetize its IP through the 9 Story platform.
    *   The market for children's content could become more saturated and competitive.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Buy. The long-term potential of the 9 Story acquisition is a compelling reason to invest, but it will require patience as the integration and synergy realization will take time.

### 7) Valuation Sensitivity Examples (short)

**1. Accelerated Share Repurchase Program**

*   **Assumption:** The company repurchases $100 million of stock at an average price of $35/share, retiring 2.86 million shares.
*   **Formula:** New EPS = Net Income / (Current Shares Outstanding - Repurchased Shares)
*   **Calculation:**
    *   Current Shares Outstanding (Common Stock as of Feb 28, 2025): 25,858,399.
    *   New Shares Outstanding: 25,858,399 - 2,860,000 = 22,998,399
    *   Assuming Net Income of $100 million:
        *   Current EPS: $100,000,000 / 25,858,399 = $3.87
        *   New EPS: $100,000,000 / 22,998,399 = $4.35 (a 12.4% increase)
*   **Valuation Impact:** With a P/E of 10x, the share price could increase from $38.70 to $43.50.

**2. 9 Story Acquisition Synergies**

*   **Assumption:** The company achieves $20 million in annual pre-tax synergies from the 9 Story acquisition.
*   **Formula:** Increase in Enterprise Value = (Synergies * (1 - Tax Rate)) / WACC
*   **Calculation:**
    *   Synergies: $20 million
    *   Tax Rate (assumed): 25%
    *   WACC (assumed): 8%
    *   Increase in Enterprise Value = ($20,000,000 * 0.75) / 0.08 = $187,500,000
*   **Valuation Impact:** An increase in enterprise value of $187.5 million would translate to a significant increase in the share price, depending on the capital structure.
