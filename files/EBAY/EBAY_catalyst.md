### **3) Near-Term Catalysts (0–12 months)**

**1. Catalyst Title:** Share Repurchase Program Execution
*   **Type:** Corporate Action
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** eBay has an ongoing stock repurchase program intended to offset dilution from equity compensation and return value to shareholders. As of March 31, 2025, $2.7 billion remained authorized for future repurchases.
*   **Mechanism:** Share buybacks reduce the number of outstanding shares, which increases earnings per share (EPS). A higher EPS, assuming a constant P/E multiple, leads to a higher stock price.
*   **Timing:** Ongoing, with the current authorization likely to be executed over the next 12-24 months.
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Case:** $1.5 billion repurchased in the next 12 months at an average price of $70/share = ~21.4 million shares retired.
    *   **Base Case:** $2.0 billion repurchased in the next 12 months at an average price of $70/share = ~28.6 million shares retired.
    *   **High Case:** $2.7 billion repurchased in the next 12 months at an average price of $70/share = ~38.6 million shares retired.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** Small (2-5%). The impact is limited by the fact that the program is already known to the market.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 90%. The company has a consistent history of executing its buyback programs.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** The company must continue to generate sufficient free cash flow to fund the repurchases.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Quarterly reports on share repurchase activity in the "Use of Proceeds" section of the 10-Q.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** A significant downturn in business could lead the company to suspend the buyback program to conserve cash.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch. Monitor the pace of buybacks each quarter.

### **4) Long-Term Catalysts (12–60 months)**

**1. Catalyst Title:** Growth in Focus Categories
*   **Type:** Operational
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** eBay's strategy includes focusing on "Focus Categories" such as Trading Cards, Motors Parts & Accessories, Luxury goods, and Refurbished items. These categories are growing faster than the rest of the marketplace.
*   **Mechanism:** Sustained growth in these higher-margin categories will lead to overall revenue growth and improved profitability, which should translate to a higher stock price.
*   **Timing:** 12-60 months. This is a multi-year strategy.
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Case:** Focus categories grow at 5% annually for the next 3 years.
    *   **Base Case:** Focus categories grow at 10% annually for the next 3 years.
    *   **High Case:** Focus categories grow at 15% annually for the next 3 years.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** Medium (10-25%). Success in this area is critical to changing the market's perception of eBay as a low-growth company.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 70%. The company has shown some early success in this area.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** eBay must continue to invest in these categories and differentiate its offering from competitors.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Management commentary on the growth of Focus Categories in quarterly earnings calls and presentations.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** Increased competition in these niche categories could erode eBay's market share and margins.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch.

### **10) Three Highest-Risk Events That Could Negate All Catalysts**

1.  **A global recession:** This would significantly impact consumer discretionary spending, which is the lifeblood of eBay's marketplace.
2.  **Increased competition from other e-commerce players:** Amazon, Walmart, and other niche marketplaces are constantly innovating and could take market share from eBay.
3.  **Regulatory action:** Changes in laws related to online marketplaces, sales tax, or data privacy could increase eBay's costs and reduce its profitability.
