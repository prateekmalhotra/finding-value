**3) Near-Term Catalysts (0–12 months)**

**1. Catalyst Title: Increased Shareholder Returns via Buybacks and Dividends**

*   **Type:** Corporate Action
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** Trip.com Group has a history of returning capital to shareholders through share buybacks and dividends. The company announced a regular capital return policy in November 2023, and there is a high probability of further authorizations and payments in the near term.
*   **Mechanism:** Share buybacks reduce the number of outstanding shares, which increases earnings per share (EPS) and can lead to a higher stock price. Dividends provide a direct return to shareholders, making the stock more attractive to income-oriented investors.
*   **Timing:** 0-12 months
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** The company announces a new $500 million share repurchase program.
    *   **Base Scenario:** A $1 billion share repurchase program is announced, along with a special dividend of $0.50 per share.
    *   **High Scenario:** A $2 billion share repurchase program and a recurring dividend policy are announced.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** 10-20%
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 80% - The company has a stated policy of returning capital to shareholders.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** Continued strong free cash flow generation.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Announcements of new share repurchase authorizations or dividend declarations in quarterly earnings reports and press releases.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** A significant downturn in the travel industry could reduce the company's cash flow and ability to fund shareholder returns.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch - Monitor for announcements of new capital return programs.

**2. Catalyst Title: Sustained Growth in Outbound Travel and International Business**

*   **Type:** Operational
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** As global travel continues to recover and expand, Trip.com Group is well-positioned to capitalize on the growth of outbound travel from China and other key markets. The company's investments in its international platforms, such as Skyscanner and Trip.com, are expected to drive significant revenue growth.
*   **Mechanism:** Increased travel demand leads to higher booking volumes and revenues. As the company's international business grows, its revenue streams will become more diversified and less reliant on the Chinese domestic market.
*   **Timing:** 0-12 months
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** International revenue grows by 15% year-over-year.
    *   **Base Scenario:** International revenue grows by 25% year-over-year.
    *   **High Scenario:** International revenue grows by 40% year-over-year, with strong margin improvement.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** 15-25%
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 75% - The global travel recovery is a strong tailwind.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** Continued easing of travel restrictions and a stable global macroeconomic environment.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Quarterly revenue growth figures, particularly for the international business segments.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** A resurgence of COVID-19 or a global recession could dampen travel demand.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Buy - The company is well-positioned to benefit from the long-term growth of global travel.
