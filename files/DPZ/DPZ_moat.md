### **1) Network Effects**
**Reasoning:** The company's vast and dense network of over 21,300 global stores creates a powerful localized network effect. A greater number of stores reduces delivery times and increases carryout convenience, which attracts more customers. This, in turn, drives sales that support franchisee investment in even greater store density. This virtuous cycle is strengthened by a large digital user base, with over 85% of U.S. retail sales coming through digital channels in 2024.
- **Evidence:**
    - As of December 29, 2024, the company had 21,366 worldwide locations, making it the largest pizza company in the world. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
    - In the U.S., more than 85% of retail sales in 2024 were generated from digital channels, which supports the Domino's Rewards loyalty program and creates a large, connected customer base. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
- **Score:** 4 / 5

### **2) Switching Costs**
**Reasoning:** While switching costs for end-customers are negligible, they are significant for the franchisees who are Domino's primary customers. Franchisees are bound by 10-year contracts, have significant store-specific capital investments, and are deeply integrated into Domino's proprietary supply chain and technology platforms like Domino's PULSE™. The extremely high franchise agreement renewal rate of approximately 99% in 2024 demonstrates the stickiness of the franchisee relationship.
- **Evidence:**
    - Standard U.S. franchise agreements have a 10-year term with a 10-year renewal option. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
    - The franchise agreement renewal rate was approximately 99% in 2024, indicating high satisfaction and lock-in. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
    - Franchisees rely on the proprietary Domino's PULSE™ point-of-sale system, which is deeply integrated into store operations. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
- **Score:** 4 / 5

### **3) Cost Advantage / Unit Cost Leadership**
**Reasoning:** Domino's scale as the world's largest pizza company gives it significant purchasing power on key inputs, most notably cheese, which represents its largest food cost. The vertically integrated supply chain, which serves over 7,600 stores in the U.S. and Canada, provides economies of scale in dough production and logistics. This system offers franchisees a cost-effective and convenient source for supplies, reinforced by a profit-sharing mechanism that further aligns interests and strengthens the cost advantage.
- **Evidence:**
    - The supply chain segment services substantially all of the more than 7,000 U.S. stores and most Canadian stores, providing economies of scale in sourcing and distribution. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
    - U.S. and Canadian franchisees who purchase from the company's supply chain centers participate in profit-sharing arrangements, receiving 50% of the pre-tax profit, which incentivizes participation and further entrenches the system's scale. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
    - A 5-year supply agreement for U.S. pizza cheese was signed in December 2024, locking in supply for a critical input. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
- **Score:** 4 / 5

### **4) Intangible Assets / Brand / IP**
**Reasoning:** The Domino's brand is a powerful intangible asset, recognized globally and equated with value, speed, and convenience. The company holds the #1 market share in the U.S. QSR pizza category, which demonstrates its brand strength. Its proprietary technology, including the Domino's PULSE™ system, acts as a key piece of intellectual property that improves operational efficiency and franchisee integration.
- **Evidence:**
    - Domino's is the largest pizza company in the world and one of the most widely-recognized consumer brands. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
    - In the U.S., Domino's holds approximately 23% market share at pizza QSRs based on consumer spending data for the year ending December 2024. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
    - The company holds many registered trademarks and has a policy to "vigorously oppose the infringement" of them. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
- **Score:** 4 / 5

### **5) Distribution & Efficient Scale**
**Reasoning:** Domino's has achieved an exceptional level of efficient scale through its dense network of stores, which makes it uneconomical for competitors to replicate. The company's "fortressing" strategy of increasing store density in existing markets shortens delivery times and enhances carryout convenience, creating a formidable barrier to entry at a local level. This dense physical network is the cornerstone of its competitive advantage in speed and service.
- **Evidence:**
    - As of December 29, 2024, the company's network consisted of 7,014 stores in the U.S. and 14,352 international stores. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
    - The company explicitly pursues a "fortressing" strategy to increase store presence in existing markets to improve service and shorten delivery areas. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
- **Score:** 5 / 5

### **6) Data Advantage**
**Reasoning:** With a vast majority of its U.S. sales originating from digital channels, Domino's accumulates a massive, proprietary dataset on customer preferences and ordering behavior. This is further enhanced by its Domino's Rewards® loyalty program. While the company possesses a significant data asset, it is less clear from public filings how effectively this data creates a durable feedback loop that materially improves the product and entrenches customers compared to competitors with similar digital platforms.
- **Evidence:**
    - In 2024, more than 85% of U.S. retail sales were derived from digital channels. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
    - The Domino's Rewards® loyalty program provides members with points for qualifying orders, creating a direct channel to gather data on its most frequent customers. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
- **Score:** 3 / 5

### **7) Ecosystem / Platform Effects**
**Reasoning:** This is not a significant source of competitive advantage for Domino's. The company's core business relies on a vertically integrated system, not a platform that connects third-party participants in a value-creating ecosystem. The recent agreement to list on Uber's marketplace is a distribution channel expansion rather than the creation of a platform with network effects.
- **Evidence:**
    - The company has a global agreement with Uber Technologies, Inc. to allow customers to order Domino's products through their marketplace. (Form 10-Q, Apr 28, 2025)
    - There is no evidence of a third-party developer ecosystem or a marketplace for complementary products and services built on a Domino's platform.
- **Score:** 1 / 5

### **8) Regulatory / Legal Barriers**
**Reasoning:** The company operates in the QSR industry, which does not benefit from significant regulatory or legal barriers to entry. Domino's is subject to standard FTC franchise regulations and local health and safety laws, but these do not provide a competitive advantage over peers. There are no proprietary licenses, patents with long-term blocking power, or government contracts that would prevent a competitor from entering the market.
- **Evidence:**
    - The company is subject to FTC rules and state laws that govern the offer and sale of franchises, which are standard for the industry. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
    - No evidence of material, exclusive licenses or regulatory approvals that restrict competition is present in the filings.
- **Score:** 1 / 5

### **9) Capital Intensity & Asset Specificity**
**Reasoning:** Domino's operates a capital-light model, a significant strength, by franchising approximately 99% of its global stores. This allows the company to expand its brand footprint and generate high-margin royalty revenue with minimal direct capital expenditure, pushing the asset intensity onto its franchisees. This asset-light structure creates a deterrent for competitors who would need to deploy immense capital to replicate the physical store network.
- **Evidence:**
    - As of December 29, 2024, approximately 99% of Domino's global stores were owned and operated by independent franchisees. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
    - This franchising strategy enables growth with "limited capital investment by us." (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
- **Score:** 5 / 5

### **10) Customer Contracts & Revenue Visibility**
**Reasoning:** The franchise model provides extremely high revenue visibility. Revenue is primarily generated from royalties based on a percentage of sales from franchisees who are locked into long-term agreements. With a renewal rate of approximately 99%, these royalty streams are highly predictable and recurring, providing a stable financial foundation for the company.
- **Evidence:**
    - U.S. franchisees enter into 10-year franchise agreements and pay a royalty fee that is generally 5.5% of sales. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
    - The franchise agreement renewal rate was approximately 99% in 2024, indicating a very stable and predictable revenue base from existing franchisees. (Form 10-K, Feb 24, 2025)
- **Score:** 5 / 5
