**F) Terminal Value**

*   **Methodology Selection:** The Gordon Growth method can be overly sensitive to inputs and, in the prior model, resulted in a conservative implied multiple. *   **Rationale for Multiple:** CNI is a premier infrastructure asset with a strong economic moat, justifying a premium valuation. It has historically traded in the 11x-13x EV/EBITDA range. We will use a multiple of **11.5x**, which sits in the lower-middle of this historical range, reflecting a mature but still high-quality business.
*   **Year 5 EBITDA:** EBIT (8,478) + D&A (2,347) = **C$10,825M**
*   **Terminal Value** = Year 5 EBITDA * Exit Multiple = C$10,825M * 11.5 = **C$124,488M**
*   **Implied Growth Cross-Check:** This terminal value implies a perpetual growth rate of **3.1%** `(g = (TV * WACC - FCFF_yr5) / (TV + FCFF_yr5))`. This rate is higher than inflation but plausible for a dominant company with pricing power, confirming that the 11.5x multiple is reasonable and not overly aggressive.
