**3) Near-Term Catalysts (0–12 months)**

**Catalyst 1**

*   **Catalyst Title:** Share Buyback Program Execution
*   **Type:** Corporate Action
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** StoneCo has a commitment to return 3 billion BRL to shareholders and has already returned 41% of this through share buybacks. The continuation of this program will reduce the number of outstanding shares and increase earnings per share.
*   **Mechanism:** A lower share count increases EPS, which, with a constant P/E multiple, leads to a higher stock price. It also signals management's confidence in the company's undervaluation.
*   **Timing:** Ongoing, with completion expected within the next 12 months.
*   **Quantification:**
    *   Low Scenario: 1 billion BRL in additional buybacks, reducing share count by ~5%.
    *   Base Scenario: 1.5 billion BRL in additional buybacks, reducing share count by ~7.5%.
    *   High Scenario: Remaining 1.77 billion BRL of the program is completed, reducing share count by ~9%.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** Medium (10-20%). A 7.5% reduction in share count, all else equal, should lead to a ~8% increase in EPS and a similar increase in the stock price, with an additional premium for the signal of undervaluation.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 90%. The company has explicitly stated its commitment and has already executed a significant portion of the program.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** The company must continue to generate sufficient free cash flow to fund the buybacks.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Quarterly reports on the progress of the buyback program, cash flow from operations, and share count.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** A significant downturn in the business could force the company to suspend the buyback program to preserve cash.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Buy. The buyback program provides a clear and tangible catalyst for share price appreciation.

**Catalyst 2**

*   **Catalyst Title:** Continued Growth in MSMB Segment
*   **Type:** Operational
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** StoneCo's Micro, Small, and Medium-sized Business (MSMB) segment is experiencing strong growth, with a 17% year-over-year increase in active payment clients to 4.5 million. Continued expansion of this client base and increased engagement will drive revenue growth.
*   **Mechanism:** A larger client base leads to higher Total Payment Volume (TPV) and, consequently, higher revenue from transaction fees and other financial services.
*   **Timing:** Ongoing, with progress reported quarterly.
*   **Quantification:**
    *   Low Scenario: 10% YoY growth in active clients and TPV.
    *   Base Scenario: 15% YoY growth in active clients and TPV.
    *   High Scenario: 20% YoY growth in active clients and TPV.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** Medium (15-25%). Sustained double-digit growth in the core MSMB segment will likely lead to a re-rating of the stock, as investors gain confidence in the long-term growth story.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 85%. The company has a strong track record of growth in this segment and continues to invest in its sales channels.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** The Brazilian economy must remain relatively stable, and StoneCo must continue to innovate and compete effectively against other fintech players.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Active client growth, TPV growth, and market share in the MSMB segment.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** Increased competition or a significant economic downturn in Brazil could slow down client acquisition and TPV growth.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Buy. The MSMB segment is the core driver of StoneCo's growth, and the current momentum is strong.

**Catalyst 3**

*   **Catalyst Title:** Successful Repricing Initiatives
*   **Type:** Financial
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** StoneCo has been implementing repricing initiatives to improve its gross profit margin. These initiatives have already started to show positive results, with a 19% year-over-year growth in gross profit in Q1 2025.
*   **Mechanism:** Higher pricing leads to improved take rates and gross profit margins, which directly translates to higher net income and a higher valuation.
*   **Timing:** 0-6 months, with the full impact expected to be reflected in the coming quarters.
*   **Quantification:**
    *   Low Scenario: 1% increase in take rate.
    *   Base Scenario: 1.5% increase in take rate.
    *   High Scenario: 2% increase in take rate.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** Small (5-15%). A 1.5% increase in the take rate could lead to a significant increase in gross profit and net income, justifying a higher valuation.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 80%. The company has already demonstrated its ability to execute on these initiatives.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** StoneCo must be able to implement the price increases without causing a significant increase in churn.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Take rate, gross profit margin, and churn rate.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** Competitors may not follow suit with price increases, leading to market share loss for StoneCo.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch. While the repricing initiatives are promising, it is important to monitor the impact on churn before making a decision.

**4) Long-Term Catalysts (12–60 months)**

**Catalyst 4**

*   **Catalyst Title:** Expansion of Credit Offerings
*   **Type:** Product
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** StoneCo is expanding its credit offerings for MSMBs, with a credit portfolio that reached BRL 1.2 billion in Q4 2024. The company sees credit as an important avenue for growth.
*   **Mechanism:** Credit products can generate significant high-margin revenue and increase customer loyalty. A successful expansion of the credit business will diversify StoneCo's revenue streams and increase its profitability.
*   **Timing:** 12-60 months. Building a large and profitable credit portfolio takes time and careful risk management.
*   **Quantification:**
    *   Low Scenario: Credit portfolio grows to BRL 3 billion in 3 years.
    *   Base Scenario: Credit portfolio grows to BRL 5 billion in 3 years.
    *   High Scenario: Credit portfolio grows to BRL 10 billion in 3 years.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** Large (>30%). A successful expansion of the credit business could double StoneCo's net income in the long term, leading to a significant re-rating of the stock.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 60%. The credit business is still in its early stages, and there are significant execution risks.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** StoneCo must effectively manage credit risk and secure adequate funding for its loan portfolio.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Growth of the credit portfolio, net interest margin, and non-performing loan ratio.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** A deterioration in the credit quality of the loan portfolio could lead to significant losses and a decline in the stock price.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch. The credit business has the potential to be a major growth driver, but it is still too early to assess the likelihood of success.
