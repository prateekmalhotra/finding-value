**Synthesis**

Alibaba scores well on its forward-looking strategy, financial strength, and depth of management. The company's strongest positives are its clear commitment to AI as the next major growth driver, backed by massive capital investment, and its fortress balance sheet, which allows it to fund growth while returning cash to shareholders. The primary negatives stem from recent instability in executive leadership and personnel, and lingering governance questions. The score is most likely to change in the next 12–24 months based on three variables: 1) the execution of its AI strategy and whether the investment translates into sustained, high-margin revenue growth in the Cloud unit; 2) stabilization of market share in the hyper-competitive domestic e-commerce segment; and 3) the stability and effectiveness of the new, more centralized leadership team under Joe Tsai and Eddie Wu. A "No" on Executive Relations would flip to "Yes" with a year of stable leadership and clear execution of the stated strategy. A "No" on Labor Relations would flip with several quarters of positive net hiring and stable employee morale metrics.
