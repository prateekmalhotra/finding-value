### Moat Duration Justification
The moat's durability is estimated at approximately **5–7 years**. This is based primarily on the strength of its intangible assets (IP and brand), which have a long shelf life. However, the core distribution model through schools faces secular pressures from digital media and changing consumer habits, which could erode its effectiveness over the medium term. The key IP assets like *Harry Potter* are enduring, but the business model that delivers them to market is less secure.

### Top 3 Moat Erosion Scenarios
1.  **Accelerated decline of in-school events:** A continued shift away from physical book fairs due to school budget cuts, administrative burden, or the rise of more efficient digital fundraising and direct-to-home purchasing could dismantle Scholastic's primary distribution advantage.
2.  **Failure to acquire new blockbuster IP:** The company's brand and revenue are heavily reliant on a few key franchises. An inability to cultivate or acquire the next generation of mega-hits could lead to brand stagnation and declining consumer interest.
3.  **Increased competition from integrated media companies:** Competitors that can more effectively bundle physical books with digital content, streaming shows, and games could capture a greater share of children's attention and spending, making Scholastic's book-centric offering seem dated.
