The original valuation was well-structured but contained several key issues that led to an overly conservative estimate. The primary flaws were:

1.  **Incorrect WACC Calculation:** A company-specific risk premium was inappropriately added to the Cost of Equity. This premium is meant for unsystematic risk, which is better accounted for in the cash flow projections, not the discount rate. Removing it results in a lower, more realistic WACC.
2.  **Overly Conservative Terminal Value:** The implied 10.1x EV/EBITDA exit multiple derived from the Gordon Growth model is too low for a high-quality, high-margin global consumer brand like Birkenstock, which should command a premium multiple even in maturity.
3.  **Subdued Growth & Margin Assumptions:** The revenue and margin assumptions, while labeled "conservative," may have been too pessimistic, underestimating the brand's pricing power, operating leverage, and momentum from direct-to-consumer (DTC) channel growth.

The following analysis corrects these issues to provide a more realistic base-case valuation.
