**3) Near-Term Catalysts (0–12 months)**

**Catalyst 1: Continued Deleveraging and Credit Rating Upgrade**

*   **Catalyst Title:** Continued Deleveraging and Credit Rating Upgrade
*   **Type:** Financial
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** The company continues to prioritize debt reduction, as evidenced by recent debt tender offers (Form 6-K, June 12, 2025) and strong free cash flow generation. A significant reduction in net debt could lead to a credit rating upgrade, lowering borrowing costs and increasing appeal to a wider range of investors.
*   **Mechanism:** A credit rating upgrade would lower the company's cost of debt, leading to higher net income and free cash flow. This would also reduce the perceived risk of the company, potentially leading to a lower discount rate in valuation models and a higher stock price.
*   **Timing:** 0-12 months
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** Net debt reduction of $5 billion. Interest expense savings of ~$200 million (assuming 4% interest rate).
    *   **Base Scenario:** Net debt reduction of $7 billion. Interest expense savings of ~$280 million.
    *   **High Scenario:** Net debt reduction of $10 billion, leading to a credit rating upgrade. Interest expense savings of >$400 million.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** Medium (10-20%)
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 85%. The company has a stated commitment to deleveraging and a strong track record of debt reduction.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** Continued strong free cash flow generation and disciplined capital allocation.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Net debt to EBITDA ratio, announcements of further debt tender offers, and credit rating agency reports (S&P, Moody's).
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** A significant economic downturn impacting cash flow, or a large acquisition that increases debt.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch.

**Catalyst 2: Margin Expansion from Premiumization and Cost Discipline**

*   **Catalyst Title:** Margin Expansion from Premiumization and Cost Discipline
*   **Type:** Operational
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** The company is successfully driving margin expansion through a focus on premium and super-premium brands, which have higher price points, and disciplined cost management. In Q2 2025, normalized EBITDA margin expanded by 116bps to 35.3% (Q2 2025 Earnings Release, July 31, 2025).
*   **Mechanism:** Higher margins translate directly to higher EBITDA and net income. If the market perceives this margin expansion as sustainable, it will likely award the company a higher valuation multiple.
*   **Timing:** 0-12 months
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** 50bps of further margin expansion on ~$60 billion of revenue = $300 million in additional EBITDA.
    *   **Base Scenario:** 100bps of further margin expansion = $600 million in additional EBITDA.
    *   **High Scenario:** 150bps of further margin expansion = $900 million in additional EBITDA.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** Medium (10-15%)
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 80%. The company has demonstrated a consistent ability to manage costs and drive premiumization.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** Consumer demand for premium beer brands remains strong, and the company can continue to find cost efficiencies.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Gross margin, EBITDA margin, and revenue growth of premium brands.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** A shift in consumer preferences away from premium brands, or a significant increase in input costs.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Buy.

**4) Long-Term Catalysts (12–60 months)**

**Catalyst 1: Growth of BEES B2B Platform**

*   **Catalyst Title:** Growth of BEES B2B Platform
*   **Type:** Operational
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** BEES is a B2B e-commerce platform that allows retailers to order beer and other products from AB InBev. The platform is growing rapidly, with Gross Merchandise Value (GMV) from third-party products up 63% to $785 million in Q2 2025 (Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript, July 31, 2025).
*   **Mechanism:** BEES creates a stickier relationship with retailers, provides a new high-margin revenue stream from third-party products, and gives AB InBev valuable data on customer purchasing habits. Over the long term, BEES could become a significant contributor to profits and be valued as a tech platform rather than a traditional beverage distribution channel.
*   **Timing:** 12-60 months
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** BEES GMV from third-party products reaches $5 billion annually, with a 5% take rate = $250 million in high-margin revenue.
    *   **Base Scenario:** BEES GMV from third-party products reaches $10 billion annually, with a 5% take rate = $500 million in high-margin revenue.
    *   **High Scenario:** BEES GMV from third-party products reaches $20 billion annually, with a 5% take rate = $1 billion in high-margin revenue.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** Medium (15-25%)
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 75%. The platform is already demonstrating strong growth, but competition and execution risks remain.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** Continued adoption of the platform by retailers, and the ability to attract and retain third-party sellers.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** BEES GMV, number of active users, and revenue from third-party sales.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** Competition from other B2B e-commerce platforms, or a failure to innovate and improve the platform.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Buy.

**10) Three Highest-Risk Events That Could Negate All Catalysts**

1.  **Global Recession:** A significant global economic downturn could lead to a decrease in beer consumption, particularly in premium brands, which would negatively impact revenue and margins.
2.  **Emerging Market Currency Devaluation:** As a global company with significant exposure to emerging markets, a sharp devaluation of key currencies could negatively impact reported earnings.
3.  **Shift in Consumer Preferences:** A significant shift in consumer preferences away from beer towards other alcoholic beverages could impact long-term growth prospects.
