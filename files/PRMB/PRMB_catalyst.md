### **3) Near-Term Catalysts (0–12 months)**

**1. Catalyst Title:** Share Repurchase Program
*   **Type:** Corporate Action
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** The Board of Directors has approved a new $250 million share repurchase program. This allows the company to buy back its own shares from the open market, reducing the number of outstanding shares.
*   **Mechanism:** A share repurchase program reduces the number of shares outstanding, which increases earnings per share (EPS). A higher EPS, assuming a constant price-to-earnings (P/E) multiple, leads to a higher stock price. It also signals management's confidence that the stock is undervalued.
*   **Timing:** August 2025 - August 2026 (12-month window for execution)
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Case:** 50% of the program is executed ($125 million).
    *   **Base Case:** 75% of the program is executed ($187.5 million).
    *   **High Case:** 100% of the program is executed ($250 million).
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** 10-20% (Medium). The repurchase program can create significant demand for the stock and signal undervaluation to the market, leading to a re-rating of the P/E multiple.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 90%. The program has been publicly announced and approved by the board.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** The company must have sufficient free cash flow to execute the repurchases without impacting its debt covenants or dividend payments.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:**
    *   Quarterly reports on share repurchase activity in the "Issuer Purchases of Equity Securities" section of 10-Q and 10-K filings.
    *   Free cash flow generation in quarterly earnings reports.
    *   Press releases announcing the initiation or completion of repurchase plans.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:**
    *   A significant downturn in business performance could reduce the cash available for repurchases.
    *   The share price could rise to a level where management no longer considers it undervalued, pausing the program.
    *   The company may prioritize debt repayment over share repurchases.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Buy. The repurchase program provides a strong support level for the stock and a clear catalyst for EPS accretion.
