### **3) Near-Term Catalysts (0–12 months)**

**1. Catalyst Title:** Share Repurchase Program

*   **Type:** Corporate Action
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** In January 2025, the Board of Directors authorized an additional $3.0 billion for the share repurchase program. As of December 31, 2024, approximately $266 million remained from the previous authorization, bringing the total available for repurchase to approximately $3.266 billion.
*   **Mechanism:** Share repurchases reduce the number of outstanding shares, which increases earnings per share (EPS). A higher EPS, assuming a constant P/E multiple, leads to a higher stock price.
*   **Timing:** The repurchase program is ongoing, with the bulk of the new authorization likely to be executed over the next 12-24 months.
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Case:** The company repurchases $1.5 billion in stock over the next 12 months.
    *   **Base Case:** The company repurchases $2.5 billion in stock over the next 12 months.
    *   **High Case:** The company repurchases the full $3.266 billion in stock over the next 12 months.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** Small (2-5%). The repurchase program is a positive signal of management's confidence in the company's future prospects and a direct way to return value to shareholders.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 95%. The company has a history of executing on its share repurchase authorizations.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** The company's stock price must be at a level that management deems attractive for repurchases.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:**
    *   Quarterly reports on share repurchase activity in the company's 10-Q and 10-K filings.
    *   Announcements of any new or expanded repurchase authorizations.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:**
    *   A significant increase in the stock price could make repurchases less attractive.
    *   The company may choose to use its cash for acquisitions or other investments instead of share repurchases.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch. The share repurchase program provides a floor for the stock price and is a positive long-term catalyst.
