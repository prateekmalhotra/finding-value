### **3) Near-Term Catalysts (0–12 months)**

**1. Catalyst Title:** Share Repurchase Program Execution
*   **Type:** Corporate Action
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** Sprouts has a $600 million share repurchase program authorized on May 22, 2024, through May 22, 2027. As of June 30, 2024, $584.6 million remained available under the program.
*   **Mechanism:** Aggressive share repurchases will reduce the number of outstanding shares, directly increasing earnings per share (EPS). This signals management's confidence in the company's undervaluation and commitment to shareholder returns, which can attract investors and drive the stock price higher.
*   **Timing:** Ongoing, with potential for acceleration in the next 0-12 months.
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** Repurchase of $200M of shares at an average price of $160/share, reducing share count by 1.25 million.
    *   **Base Scenario:** Repurchase of $400M of shares at an average price of $160/share, reducing share count by 2.5 million.
    *   **High Scenario:** Repurchase of the full remaining $584.6M at an average price of $160/share, reducing share count by 3.65 million.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** Medium (10-20%). A significant reduction in share count can lead to a direct increase in EPS, and assuming a constant P/E multiple, a corresponding increase in the share price.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 90%. The company has a history of executing on its share repurchase authorizations and has explicitly stated its intention to continue to do so opportunistically.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** Continued strong free cash flow generation and a belief by management that the stock is attractively priced.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:**
    *   Quarterly reports on share repurchase activity in Form 10-Q and 10-K filings.
    *   Daily/weekly stock price and volume for signs of large block purchases.
    *   Company announcements of any accelerated share repurchase (ASR) programs.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:**
    *   A significant increase in the stock price could make repurchases less attractive.
    *   Management may choose to allocate capital to other priorities, such as acquisitions.
    *   A downturn in business performance could reduce the cash available for repurchases.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch. Monitor the pace of share repurchases as an indicator of management's confidence.

**2. Catalyst Title:** Continued Comparable Store Sales Growth and Positive Earnings Surprises
*   **Type:** Financial
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** Sprouts has been consistently beating earnings expectations, with a Q1 2025 EPS of $1.81 versus expectations of $1.54, and Q2 2025 EPS of $1.35 versus expectations of $1.23. The company has guided for continued strong sales growth in 2025.
*   **Mechanism:** Continued strong comparable store sales growth and earnings beats will lead to upward revisions of analyst estimates and increased investor confidence, driving the stock price higher.
*   **Timing:** Next 1-4 quarters (next earnings reports).
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** Comparable store sales growth at the low end of guidance (5.5%).
    *   **Base Scenario:** Comparable store sales growth in the middle of guidance (6.5%).
    *   **High Scenario:** Comparable store sales growth at the high end of guidance (7.5%) or exceeding it.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** Small to Medium (5-15%). Positive earnings surprises are often rewarded with a short-term stock price increase.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 70%. The company has a strong track record of recent performance, but the retail environment is competitive.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** Successful execution of merchandising and marketing initiatives, and a stable macroeconomic environment.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:**
    *   Quarterly earnings releases and comparable store sales growth figures.
    *   Company guidance updates.
    *   Foot traffic data and reports.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:**
    *   Increased competition from other grocery retailers.
    *   A slowdown in consumer spending due to economic factors.
    *   Failure to innovate and keep up with changing consumer preferences.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Buy. The company's strong momentum and potential for continued outperformance make it an attractive investment.

### **4) Long-Term Catalysts (12–60 months)**

*   **Type:** Operational
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** Sprouts is focused on expanding its store base by approximately 10% annually, with at least 35 new stores planned for 2025, all in a smaller, more efficient format. This new format is expected to deliver stronger returns on investment.
*   **Mechanism:** The successful rollout of new stores will drive top-line revenue growth and market share gains. The smaller format is designed for higher profitability, which will improve the company's overall margin profile and free cash flow generation over the long term.
*   **Timing:** 12-60 months.
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** 8% annual unit growth with new stores performing at the low end of expectations.
    *   **Base Scenario:** 10% annual unit growth with new stores performing in line with expectations.
    *   **High Scenario:** 12% or more annual unit growth with new stores exceeding performance expectations.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** Large (>30%). Successful expansion is a key driver of long-term value creation for retailers.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 85%. The company has a clear expansion strategy and a proven track record of opening new stores.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** Ability to secure attractive real estate locations, manage the supply chain for a larger store base, and maintain a strong brand image in new markets.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:**
    *   Quarterly and annual reports on the number of new store openings.
    *   Company presentations and investor calls discussing new store performance.
    *   Geographic sales data to assess performance in new markets.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:**
    *   Delays in new store openings due to construction or permitting issues.
    *   Poor performance of new stores in certain markets.
    *   Increased competition in target expansion markets.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Buy. The long-term growth story is a primary reason to invest in SFM.

### **10) Three Highest-Risk Events That Could Negate All Catalysts**

1.  **Severe Economic Downturn:** A significant recession could lead to a sharp decline in consumer spending, even on grocery items, and a shift to lower-priced competitors, negating sales growth and margin expansion efforts.
2.  **Major Food Safety Incident:** A widespread food safety recall or incident, especially involving a Sprouts Brand product, could severely damage the company's reputation and brand, leading to a loss of customer trust and a significant decline in sales.
3.  **Intensified Competition:** Increased competition from both conventional and specialty grocery retailers could lead to a price war, eroding margins and making it more difficult for Sprouts to achieve its growth targets.
