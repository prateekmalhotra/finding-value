### **3) Near-Term Catalysts (0–12 months)**

**1. Catalyst Title:** Development Pipeline Completion and Lease-Up

*   **Type:** Operational
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** MAA is expected to complete and stabilize seven new development projects, totaling 2,312 units, which will begin contributing to revenue and NOI growth. Three projects with a total of 1,069 units were already completed in 2024.
*   **Mechanism:** As these new units are leased up, they will directly increase rental revenue and net operating income (NOI), which will in turn increase funds from operations (FFO) per share. Higher FFO per share is a primary driver of REIT valuations and will likely lead to a higher stock price.
*   **Timing:** The projects have expected completion dates ranging from Q3 2025 to Q4 2027.
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** Average rent per unit of $1,800, 90% occupancy, and 60% NOI margin.
    *   **Base Scenario:** Average rent per unit of $2,000, 93% occupancy, and 65% NOI margin.
    *   **High Scenario:** Average rent per unit of $2,200, 95% occupancy, and 70% NOI margin.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** 10-15%.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 90%. The projects are already under construction and MAA has a strong track record of successful development.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** Successful and timely completion of construction, and a stable or growing rental market in the properties' locations.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Construction progress reports, lease-up velocity, and rental rate achievement compared to underwriting.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** Construction delays, cost overruns, and weaker-than-expected rental demand.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch.

**2. Catalyst Title:** Share Repurchase Program

*   **Type:** Corporate Action
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** MAA has a share repurchase program with 4.0 million shares remaining under authorization.
*   **Mechanism:** Share repurchases reduce the number of outstanding shares, which increases earnings per share (EPS) and FFO per share. This can also signal to the market that management believes the stock is undervalued, which can attract buyers.
*   **Timing:** The company may repurchase shares at any time.
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** 1 million shares repurchased.
    *   **Base Scenario:** 2 million shares repurchased.
    *   **High Scenario:** 4 million shares repurchased.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** 5-10%.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 60%. The company has not recently been active in the market, but the authorization is in place.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** The stock price trading at a level that management considers attractive for buybacks.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Company announcements of share repurchase activity and the stock's price-to-FFO multiple.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** The company may choose to use capital for other purposes, such as acquisitions or development.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch.

*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** Availability of attractive acquisition targets at reasonable prices.

*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** Overpaying for acquisitions, or a downturn in the rental market in the newly entered locations.

*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** Continued ability to identify properties with redevelopment potential and to execute renovations on time and on budget.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Number of units renovated, cost per renovation, and rental rate uplift on renovated units.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** A downturn in the rental market could reduce the ROI on renovations.
