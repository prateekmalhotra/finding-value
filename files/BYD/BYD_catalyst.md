### **3) Near-Term Catalysts (0–12 months)**

**1. Catalyst Title:** Aggressive Execution of Share Repurchase Program

*   **Type:** Corporate Action
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** The company has an active and recently expanded share repurchase program. As of June 30, 2025, there was $207.5 million remaining under the authorization.
*   **Mechanism:** Share repurchases reduce the number of outstanding shares, which increases earnings per share (EPS). A higher EPS, assuming a constant P/E multiple, leads to a higher stock price. It also signals management's belief that the stock is undervalued.
*   **Timing:** Ongoing, with potential for acceleration in the next 0-12 months.
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** Repurchase of $100 million at an average price of $85/share, retiring ~1.18 million shares.
    *   **Base Scenario:** Repurchase of $207.5 million at an average price of $80/share, retiring ~2.59 million shares.
    *   **High Scenario:** Repurchase of the full remaining authorization plus a potential new authorization, totaling $400 million at an average price of $75/share, retiring ~5.33 million shares.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** Medium (5-15%). The base case of retiring ~2.59 million shares would reduce the share count by ~3.2%, directly boosting EPS by the same amount. The signal of confidence and continued reduction in share count could add a premium, leading to a 5-10% price impact.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 90%. The company has a consistent track record of share repurchases, as detailed in its recent 10-Q filings.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** Continued strong free cash flow generation and a stock price that management deems attractive for repurchases.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:**
    *   Quarterly reports on Form 10-Q, Item 2, for "Issuer Purchases of Equity Securities."
    *   Press releases announcing any new or expanded repurchase authorizations.
    *   Daily trading volume and price to assess periods of opportunistic buybacks.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:**
    *   A significant acquisition or downturn in business could divert cash flow away from repurchases.
    *   A rapid increase in the stock price could make repurchases less attractive.
    *   Insider selling pressure, as evidenced by recent transactions, could partially offset the impact of buybacks.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch. Monitor the pace of repurchases in the upcoming quarterly filings.

**2. Catalyst Title:** Successful Opening of Transitional Norfolk Casino

*   **Type:** Operational / Product
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** Boyd Gaming is developing a new casino resort in Norfolk, Virginia, with a transitional facility planned to open in late 2025. This will be the company's first entry into the Virginia market.
*   **Mechanism:** The opening of a new property, even a temporary one, generates new revenue and EBITDA streams. It also de-risks the larger project and provides a tangible milestone for the market to value, likely leading to multiple expansion.
*   **Timing:** Late 2025.
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** Transitional facility generates $20 million in annualized EBITDA.
    *   **Base Scenario:** Transitional facility generates $40 million in annualized EBITDA.
    *   **High Scenario:** Transitional facility generates $60 million in annualized EBITDA.
    *   *Inference:* These numbers are estimates based on the performance of other regional gaming properties. The full resort is expected to be a much larger contributor.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** Small (0-10%). While the full resort is a major catalyst, the transitional facility's opening is a significant milestone. A successful opening could lead to a 5-10% increase in the stock price as the market begins to price in the full project's potential.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 85%. The project is underway, and the company has provided a clear timeline.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** Obtaining all necessary regulatory approvals and completing construction on schedule.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:**
    *   Company press releases on the construction progress.
    *   Local news reports from Norfolk, Virginia, regarding the project.
    *   Management commentary on earnings calls.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:**
    *   Construction delays or cost overruns.
    *   Regulatory hurdles.
    *   Weaker-than-expected initial performance.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Buy. The market may not be fully pricing in the impact of this new market entry.
