**3) Near-Term Catalysts (0–12 months)**

**Catalyst 1: Continued Passenger Traffic Recovery**

*   **Catalyst Title:** Continued Passenger Traffic Recovery
*   **Type:** Operational
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** A sustained recovery in passenger traffic across CAAP's key markets, particularly in Argentina, Brazil, and Italy, will drive revenue and earnings growth. The company reported a 6.0% year-over-year increase in passenger traffic in February 2025. (Press Release, March 18, 2025)
*   **Mechanism:** Higher passenger traffic directly increases aeronautical revenues (passenger fees) and non-aeronautical revenues (retail, parking, etc.). This top-line growth, with a largely fixed cost base, should lead to margin expansion and higher profitability, which in turn would increase the company's valuation.
*   **Timing:** August 2025 – August 2026
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** 5% increase in passenger traffic.
    *   **Base Scenario:** 10% increase in passenger traffic.
    *   **High Scenario:** 15% increase in passenger traffic.
    *   *Note: These are estimates based on the limited data available.*
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** 10-20% (Medium)
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 80%. Rationale: The post-pandemic travel recovery is a strong global trend.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** Stable global economic conditions, no new travel restrictions, and continued airline capacity growth.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Monthly passenger traffic reports, airline earnings reports, and global travel data.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** A global recession, a new pandemic, or a sharp increase in oil prices could all negatively impact travel demand.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch. Monitor monthly passenger traffic data for signs of continued growth.

**Catalyst 2: Increased Ownership of Italian Subsidiary**

*   **Catalyst Title:** Increased Ownership of Italian Subsidiary
*   **Type:** M&A / Corporate Action
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** On May 28, 2025, CAAP acquired the remaining 25% of Corporación América Italia S.p.A. from the Investment Corporation of Dubai, giving it 100% ownership.
*   **Mechanism:** This transaction will be accretive to CAAP's earnings per share, as the company will no longer have to share the profits from its Italian operations with a minority shareholder. It also simplifies the corporate structure and gives CAAP full control over the Italian business.
*   **Timing:** May 2025 – November 2025
*   **Quantification:**
    *   *Unable to provide a detailed quantitative analysis without access to the full financial statements.*
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** 5-10% (Small)
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 95%. The transaction has already been completed.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** Successful integration of the remaining stake and continued strong performance of the Italian airports.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Quarterly financial reports (to see the impact on earnings), and passenger traffic data for the Italian airports.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** A downturn in the Italian economy could negatively impact the performance of the Italian airports.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Buy. This is a positive, completed transaction that should be accretive to earnings.

**4) Long-Term Catalysts (12–60 months)**

**Catalyst 3: Favorable Regulatory Developments in Argentina**

*   **Catalyst Title:** Favorable Regulatory Developments in Argentina
*   **Type:** Regulatory
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** The new government in Argentina could implement more market-friendly policies, which could lead to a more favorable regulatory environment for CAAP's Argentine operations, its largest market.
*   **Mechanism:** More favorable regulations could include longer concession agreements, higher tariffs, and a more stable economic environment. This would reduce risk and increase the long-term value of CAAP's Argentine business, leading to a higher stock price.
*   **Timing:** August 2026 – August 2030
*   **Quantification:**
    *   *Unable to provide a detailed quantitative analysis without more information on potential regulatory changes.*
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** >30% (Large)
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 40%. Rationale: Political and regulatory changes are difficult to predict.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** The new government must be willing and able to implement market-friendly policies.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** News and announcements from the Argentine government regarding airport concessions, economic policy, and foreign investment.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** The new government may not be successful in implementing its policies, or its policies may not be favorable to CAAP. Argentina has a long history of political and economic instability.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch. This is a high-impact, low-probability catalyst.
