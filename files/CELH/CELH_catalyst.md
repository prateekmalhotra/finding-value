### **3) Near-Term Catalysts (0–12 months)**

**1. Catalyst Title:** Margin Improvement from Vertical Integration
*   **Type:** Operational
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** On November 1, 2024, Celsius acquired Big Beverages, a co-packer, for $75 million in cash. This acquisition is a strategic move towards vertical integration, which could lead to improved control over the supply chain and reduced production costs.
*   **Mechanism:** By insourcing a portion of its manufacturing, Celsius can potentially reduce its cost of goods sold (COGS), leading to higher gross profit margins. This would, in turn, increase net income and earnings per share (EPS), driving the stock price higher.
*   **Timing:** 6-18 months (integration and realization of synergies)
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** 1% improvement in gross margin. Based on LTM revenue of ~$1.8B, this would result in an additional $18M in gross profit.
    *   **Base Scenario:** 2% improvement in gross margin, resulting in an additional $36M in gross profit.
    *   **High Scenario:** 3% improvement in gross margin, resulting in an additional $54M in gross profit.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** Medium (+10-20%). A $36M increase in gross profit, at a 25x P/E multiple, could add approximately $900M to the company's market capitalization.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 75%. The acquisition has been completed, but the full extent of the synergies and margin improvement is yet to be realized.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** Successful integration of Big Beverages' operations and a smooth transition to in-house production.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Gross profit margin trends in upcoming quarterly reports, management commentary on the integration process, and any updates on production capacity.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** Integration challenges, unexpected costs, or an inability to achieve the desired level of efficiency and cost savings.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch. Monitor gross margins and management commentary closely.

**2. Catalyst Title:** Resolution of Strong Arm Productions Lawsuit
*   **Type:** Legal
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** Celsius is involved in a lawsuit with Strong Arm Productions, which resulted in an $82.6 million judgment against the company. The company is appealing the verdict and has accrued a liability at the low end of the range of possible outcomes ($2.1 million).
*   **Mechanism:** A favorable resolution, such as a reversal of the verdict or a settlement at the low end of the range, would remove a significant overhang on the stock and could lead to a reversal of the accrued liability, boosting net income. Conversely, an unfavorable outcome could result in a large cash payment, impacting the balance sheet and investor sentiment.
*   **Timing:** 0-12 months
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario (Negative):** Upholding of the full $82.6 million judgment, resulting in a one-time charge of ~$80M.
    *   **Base Scenario (Neutral):** Settlement in the range of $10-20 million.
    *   **High Scenario (Positive):** Reversal of the verdict or settlement at the accrued amount of $2.1 million.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** Medium (+/-10-20%). The outcome of this lawsuit could swing the stock price in either direction.
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 60%. The outcome of legal proceedings is inherently uncertain.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** The decision of the appellate court.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Any news or press releases regarding the lawsuit, updates in the company's 10-Q and 10-K filings, and any changes to the accrued liability on the balance sheet.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** A final verdict at the high end of the range, which would negatively impact cash reserves and earnings.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch. The binary nature of this event makes it a high-risk, high-reward catalyst.
