**3) Near-Term Catalysts (0–12 months)**

**Catalyst 1**
*   **Catalyst Title:** Share Repurchase Program Execution
*   **Type:** Corporate Action
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** The company has an active share repurchase program, which was replenished to $200.0 million and extended indefinitely as of February 2, 2024. As of September 29, 2024, the remaining balance of the repurchase program was $156.7 million.
*   **Mechanism:** Aggressive execution of the share repurchase program will reduce the number of outstanding shares, directly increasing earnings per share (EPS). This signals management's belief that the stock is undervalued and can create buying pressure, driving the share price higher.
*   **Timing:** Ongoing, with potential for accelerated repurchases in the next 0-12 months.
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Case:** 25% of the remaining authorization used ($39.2 million) to repurchase ~3.5 million shares at an average price of $11.00.
    *   **Base Case:** 50% of the remaining authorization used ($78.4 million) to repurchase ~7.1 million shares at an average price of $11.00.
    *   **High Case:** 75% of the remaining authorization used ($117.5 million) to repurchase ~10.7 million shares at an average price of $11.00.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** 10-20%
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 85%. The company has a consistent track record of executing share repurchases.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** The company must continue to generate sufficient free cash flow to fund repurchases without compromising its growth initiatives.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:**
    *   Quarterly reports on Form 10-Q and annual reports on Form 10-K for updates on the number of shares repurchased and the remaining authorization.
    *   Insider trading filings to monitor for any changes in management's buying or selling activity.
    *   Press releases announcing any further replenishments or modifications to the repurchase program.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:**
    *   A significant downturn in the business could reduce the cash available for repurchases.
    *   The company may choose to prioritize acquisitions or other capital expenditures over share repurchases.
    *   A sharp increase in the stock price could make repurchases less attractive.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Watch. Monitor the pace of share repurchases in upcoming quarterly reports.

**Catalyst 2**
*   **Catalyst Title:** New Location Openings and Acquisitions
*   **Type:** Operational
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** The company is actively expanding its footprint through both new build-outs and acquisitions. There are six signed agreements for new locations, with four expected to open in the second quarter.
*   **Mechanism:** The opening of new locations and the successful integration of acquisitions will drive top-line revenue growth. As these new locations mature and reach their full earnings potential, they will contribute to higher overall profitability and cash flow.
*   **Timing:** New locations are expected to open in the second quarter of the fiscal year. Acquisitions are ongoing.
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Case:** Successful opening of the four planned new locations, contributing an estimated $20-30 million in annualized revenue.
    *   **Base Case:** Successful opening of all six planned new locations and the completion of 1-2 small acquisitions, contributing an estimated $40-60 million in annualized revenue.
    *   **High Case:** Successful opening of all six planned new locations and the completion of a larger, more strategic acquisition, contributing over $80 million in annualized revenue.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** 15-25%
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 75%. The company has a proven track record of successfully opening new locations and integrating acquisitions.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** The company must successfully execute on its new build-out plans and effectively integrate any acquired businesses.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:**
    *   Press releases announcing new location openings and acquisitions.
    *   Quarterly and annual reports for updates on the number of locations and their financial performance.
    *   Management commentary on earnings calls regarding the pipeline for new locations and acquisitions.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:**
    *   Delays in construction or permitting for new locations.
    *   Difficulties in integrating acquired businesses.
    *   Overpaying for acquisitions, leading to a lower-than-expected return on investment.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Buy. The company's expansion plans provide a clear path to future growth.

*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** The company must continue to generate sufficient free cash flow to support the dividend and any future increases.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:**

    *   Management commentary on earnings calls regarding the company's dividend policy.

*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:**
