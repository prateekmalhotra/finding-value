**3) Near-Term Catalysts (0–12 months)**

**1. Catalyst Title:** Aggressive Share Repurchases Under New Authorization
*   **Type:** Corporate Action
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** On March 5, 2025, the Board of Directors authorized a new $1.3 billion share repurchase program, replacing the previous $500 million authorization. This new authorization signals a strong commitment to returning capital to shareholders.
*   **Mechanism:** Share repurchases reduce the number of outstanding shares, which increases earnings per share (EPS). A higher EPS, assuming a constant P/E multiple, leads to a higher stock price. Aggressive repurchases can also signal management's confidence in the company's future prospects.
*   **Timing:** August 2025 – August 2026
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** Repurchase of $500 million of stock. At an assumed average price of $150/share, this would retire ~3.3 million shares, or ~6.7% of shares outstanding.
    *   **Base Scenario:** Repurchase of $800 million of stock. At an assumed average price of $150/share, this would retire ~5.3 million shares, or ~10.7% of shares outstanding.
    *   **High Scenario:** Repurchase of $1.1 billion of stock. At an assumed average price of $150/share, this would retire ~7.3 million shares, or ~14.7% of shares outstanding.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** 10-20%
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 90%. The company has a history of completing share repurchase authorizations and the new authorization is substantial.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** The company must continue to generate strong free cash flow to fund the repurchases. The stock price must remain at a level where management deems repurchases to be an attractive use of capital.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Quarterly reports on share repurchase activity in the company's 10-Q and 10-K filings.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** A significant downturn in the business could reduce free cash flow and a rapid increase in the stock price could make repurchases less attractive.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Buy. The new repurchase authorization provides a strong near-term catalyst.

**2. Catalyst Title:** Continued Gross Margin Expansion
*   **Type:** Financial
*   **Description (1–2 sentences):** The company has been successful in increasing its gross margin through a combination of lower promotional activity and reduced product costs. Continued discipline in this area could lead to further margin expansion.
*   **Mechanism:** Higher gross margins translate directly to higher operating income and net income. This can lead to a higher stock price through improved profitability and a potential re-rating of the company's valuation multiple.
*   **Timing:** August 2025 – August 2026
*   **Quantification:**
    *   **Low Scenario:** 50 basis point improvement in gross margin. On Fiscal 2024 revenue of $4.95 billion, this would add ~$25 million to gross profit.
    *   **Base Scenario:** 100 basis point improvement in gross margin. On Fiscal 2024 revenue of $4.95 billion, this would add ~$50 million to gross profit.
    *   **High Scenario:** 150 basis point improvement in gross margin. On Fiscal 2024 revenue of $4.95 billion, this would add ~$75 million to gross profit.
*   **Estimated Price Impact:** 10-25%
*   **Probability / Confidence (0–100%):** 80%. The company has demonstrated its ability to manage promotions and product costs effectively.
*   **Key Dependencies / Conditions:** The company must maintain its brand strength to avoid a return to heavy promotional activity. Input costs must remain stable or decline.
*   **Monitoring Triggers / KPIs:** Gross margin reported in the company's quarterly earnings releases and 10-Q filings.
*   **Anti-Catalysts / Risks:** Increased competition could force a return to a more promotional environment. Inflation in input costs could pressure margins.
*   **Suggested Investor Action:** Buy. The trend of improving gross margins is a strong positive for the company's profitability.
