Here is an analysis of Micron Technology, Inc. (MU) using Philip A. Fisher’s 15-point checklist.

**Company, date of analysis, primary sources reviewed.**
Micron Technology, Inc. (MU), August 24, 2025, primary sources reviewed: Form 10-K filed for the fiscal year ended August 29, 2024, and Forms 10-Q filed for quarterly periods ending November 28, 2024, February 27, 2025, and May 29, 2025.

**1) Products/Services with Long Runway**
**Question:** Does the company have products or services with sufficient market potential to make possible a sizable increase in sales for at least several years?
**Reasoning & Evidence:** Micron's primary products, DRAM and NAND, are fundamental to major long-term growth trends including artificial intelligence (AI), 5G, and the intelligent edge. The company's 2024 10-K highlights that cloud servers supporting AI and data-centric workloads require significantly increasing quantities of DRAM, including High-Bandwidth Memory (HBM), and NAND. For example, Micron began volume production of its HBM3E, which is ideal for AI applications, in 2024. The proliferation of AI into PCs and smartphones is also expected to drive significant increases in memory and storage content per device.
**Answer:** Yes
**Citations:** https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/723125/000072312524000027/mu-20240829.htm (August 29, 2024)

**2) Commitment to Ongoing Development**
**Question:** Does management have a determination to continue to develop products or processes that will further increase total sales potential once current lines mature?
**Reasoning & Evidence:** Micron is heavily investing in next-generation technology and manufacturing capacity. The company's R&D efforts are focused on the development of 1-gamma DRAM, which will use EUV lithography, with volume production on track for 2025. Micron is also making significant capital expenditures, with plans to build new leading-edge memory manufacturing fabs in Boise, Idaho, and Clay, New York, supported by the U.S. CHIPS and Science Act. These investments are aimed at meeting the projected memory demand in the second half of the decade and beyond.
**Answer:** Yes
**Citations:** https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/723125/000072312524000027/mu-20240829.htm (August 29, 2024)

**3) R&D Effectiveness Relative to Size**
**Question:** How effective are the company’s R&D efforts in relation to its size?
**Reasoning & Evidence:** Micron's R&D expenses were $3.43 billion in fiscal year 2024, representing approximately 14% of revenue. These investments have led to significant product innovations, such as the industry's first 1-beta DRAM production node and 232-layer NAND. Micron's progress in HBM3E and its 1-gamma DRAM pilot production also demonstrate effective R&D outcomes. As of August 29, 2024, Micron owned approximately 14,500 active U.S. patents and 7,000 active foreign patents, reflecting a strong intellectual property portfolio.
**Answer:** Yes
**Citations:** https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/723125/000072312524000027/mu-20240829.htm (August 29, 2024)

**4) Above-Average Sales Organization**
**Question:** Does the company have an above-average sales organization?
**Reasoning & Evidence:** Micron markets its products through a direct sales force, distributors, retailers, and web-based channels under the Micron and Crucial brands. The company emphasizes building collaborative relationships with customers early in the product life-cycle to design products that meet their changing needs. In fiscal 2024, approximately half of Micron's revenue came from its top ten customers, indicating strong relationships with key players in the industry. While a concentration of customers can be a risk, it also reflects the success of the sales organization in securing large, long-term accounts.

**5) Worthwhile Profit Margins**
**Question:** Does the company have a worthwhile profit margin?
**Reasoning & Evidence:** The semiconductor memory industry is cyclical, leading to volatile profit margins. In fiscal 2024, Micron's gross margin was 22%. This is a significant improvement from fiscal 2023, where the gross margin was -9% due to a market downturn. For the quarter ending May 29, 2025, Micron reported a gross margin of 38%, showing a strong recovery. While margins are subject to industry cycles, the recent trend indicates a return to profitability.
**Answer:** Yes

**Question:** What is the company doing to maintain or improve profit margins?

The company is also committed to cost reduction through the transition to more advanced process nodes, such as 1-gamma for DRAM and next-generation 3D NAND. In 2023, in response to a market downturn, Micron implemented a restructure plan that reduced its headcount and discretionary spending, which is estimated to yield significant cost savings.

Synthesis:
Micron Technology scores highly on the Fisher checklist, reflecting its strong position in a critical and growing industry. The company's key strengths lie in its technology leadership, commitment to long-term R&D and capacity expansion, and a stable and experienced management team. The cyclical nature of the memory industry remains a key variable, as evidenced by the significant swing in profit margins between 2023 and 2025. The most likely factors to change the score in the next 12-24 months are the execution of its technology roadmap, particularly the ramp-up of 1-gamma DRAM and HBM products, and the successful execution of its ambitious fab expansion plans in the U.S. Additionally, the sustainability of the current upcycle in the memory market will be a critical factor for the company's profitability and ability to fund its growth plans without straining its financial position.
