This is a good and well-structured valuation analysis. It correctly identifies the core challenge with Lumen: its enormous debt load makes the equity value incredibly sensitive to future assumptions. However, there are several critical issues in the methodology that, when corrected, significantly alter the conclusion.

The primary flaws in the original analysis are:

1.  **Incorrect Cost of Debt:** The analysis uses the *effective interest rate* (Interest Expense / Total Debt) to calculate the Cost of Debt. For a company with significant credit risk like Lumen, the correct input is the *current market cost of debt*, which reflects the yield-to-maturity (YTM) on its outstanding bonds. This market rate is invariably higher than the historical effective rate and better reflects the true cost of capital for new financing or refinancing.
2.  **Resulting WACC is Too Low:** Because the Cost of Debt was understated, the calculated WACC of 7.26% is too low for a company with Lumen's risk profile. A higher, more accurate WACC will reduce the present value of future cash flows, putting further pressure on the valuation.
3.  **Terminal Value Justification:** The original analysis correctly identifies that the Gordon Growth implied multiple is too low and rightly switches to an Exit Multiple. However, the process of arriving at the 6.0x multiple felt like it was chosen to produce a positive result. While 6.0x may be reasonable, the justification can be strengthened by anchoring it to industry norms and the company's specific risk profile.

Below is a revised valuation that corrects these flaws, provides more realistic assumptions, and maintains the same clear format.
