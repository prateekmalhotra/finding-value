**1) Network Effects**
- **Reasoning:** McDonald's is developing a digital network effect through its loyalty program. As more customers join the loyalty program, the company gathers more data to personalize offers, which in turn attracts more users and increases visit frequency. The franchisee network also represents a network effect, as the success and density of one store can lift the brand value and operational knowledge for others.
- **Evidence:**
    - The company plans to increase its 90-day active loyalty users to 250 million by the end of 2027, up from successful programs in 60 markets. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
    - McDonald's aims to grow annual Systemwide sales to loyalty members to $45.0 billion by the end of 2027. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
- **Score:** 3 / 5

**2) Switching Costs**
- **Reasoning:** For consumers, switching costs are negligible. However, for franchisees, switching costs are exceptionally high, creating a powerful lock-in effect. Franchisees make substantial, specific capital investments and are bound by long-term franchise agreements (typically 20 years), making it financially prohibitive to rebrand or switch affiliations.
- **Evidence:**
    - Franchise/license agreements generally have 20-year terms. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
    - Under a conventional franchise, the franchisee pays for equipment, signs, seating, and décor, representing a significant upfront investment. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
    - As of December 31, 2024, approximately 95% of the 43,477 McDonald's restaurants were franchised, demonstrating the scale of this high-switching-cost model. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
- **Score:** 4 / 5

**3) Cost Advantage / Unit Cost Leadership**
- **Reasoning:** McDonald's possesses a formidable cost advantage derived from its immense global scale. This scale allows for superior bargaining power with suppliers for food, packaging, and equipment, leading to lower per-unit costs than competitors. This advantage is structural and difficult for any competitor to replicate.
- **Evidence:**
    - The company's size and scale allow it to "harness its competitive advantages" to adapt to varying economic environments, including inflationary pressures. (Form 10-Q, Aug 06, 2025).
    - The company's supply chain management assures "a reliable and sufficient supply of quality products, equipment and other materials on favorable terms." (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
    - Franchised margins represented approximately 90% of total restaurant margin dollars in 2024, indicating the cost-efficient and stable revenue stream from the franchised model. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
- **Score:** 4 / 5

**4) Intangible Assets / Brand / IP**
- **Reasoning:** McDonald's possesses one of the most valuable and recognized brands in the world, built over decades of consistent marketing and service. This powerful brand allows it to attract customers and franchisees, command cultural relevance, and execute global marketing campaigns with unmatched efficiency. The "McDonald's" trademark and Golden Arches Logo are explicitly cited as being of material importance to the business.
- **Evidence:**
    - The company's "Accelerating the Arches" strategy relies on maximizing marketing by investing in "new, culturally relevant approaches... to effectively communicate the story of our brand, food and purpose." (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
    - The company considers the "McDonald's" trademark and the Golden Arches Logo to be of material importance to its business. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
    - The company highlights core menu items like the Big Mac, Quarter Pounder, and Chicken McNuggets as "seventeen unique billion-dollar brands." (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
- **Score:** 5 / 5

**5) Distribution & Efficient Scale**
- **Reasoning:** McDonald's has an unparalleled global distribution network of restaurants in prime locations, creating a significant barrier to entry. With over 44,000 locations, the density of its network provides unmatched convenience to customers and makes it exceedingly difficult for competitors to gain a comparable physical foothold. This scale makes the market unattractive for new entrants to challenge directly.
- **Evidence:**
    - At June 30, 2025, there were 44,113 Systemwide restaurants across more than 100 countries. (Form 10-Q, Aug 06, 2025).
    - The company has over 28,000 drive-thru locations globally, including over 95% of its 13,500+ locations in the U.S., which it cites as a key competitive advantage. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
    - The company plans to accelerate development toward a target of 50,000 restaurants by the end of 2027, the fastest growth in its history. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
- **Score:** 5 / 5

**6) Data Advantage**
- **Reasoning:** McDonald's is rapidly building a significant data advantage through its digital and loyalty programs. The data collected on the purchasing habits of millions of customers allows for increasingly effective personalized marketing, menu optimization, and operational improvements. This creates a feedback loop where more data leads to better customer experiences, driving further engagement and data collection.
- **Evidence:**
    - The company is "building one of the world's largest consumer platforms to fuel engagement" which will accelerate growth in the loyalty program. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
    - McDonald's plans to increase its 90-day active loyalty users to 250 million by the end of 2027. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
    - The company is deploying "Ready on Arrival" technology in its top six markets, which uses digital data to have orders ready upon customer arrival, enhancing convenience. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
- **Score:** 3 / 5

**7) Ecosystem / Platform Effects**
- **Reasoning:** McDonald's has a nascent ecosystem primarily centered on its delivery partnerships. While it partners with major third-party delivery services, it does not own the platform or have a significant number of third-party developers building on its technology. This dimension is currently a minor source of competitive advantage.
- **Evidence:**
    - The company has long-term strategic partnerships with delivery providers for its 39,000+ restaurants offering delivery. (Form 10-Q, Aug 06, 2025).
    - The company is increasing the ability to place delivery orders through its own mobile app, aiming for 30% of Systemwide delivery sales to originate there by 2027. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
- **Score:** 1 / 5

**8) Regulatory / Legal Barriers**
- **Reasoning:** McDonald's must comply with extensive food safety, franchising, and employment regulations worldwide, but these regulations do not provide a significant barrier to entry for competitors. While complex, these are costs of doing business in the industry rather than a unique advantage for McDonald's. Its scale may provide some advantage in navigating this complexity, but it is not a primary moat source.
- **Evidence:**
    - The company is subject to laws and regulations involving advertising, franchising, health, safety, environment, competition, zoning, and employment. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
    - The business is exposed to litigation and regulatory action concerning its relationship with franchisees, which it identifies as a risk factor. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
- **Score:** 2 / 5

**9) Capital Intensity & Asset Specificity**
- **Reasoning:** The capital required to build a global restaurant chain with a comparable real estate footprint is immense, creating a strong barrier to entry. McDonald's generally owns or secures long-term leases on the land and buildings for its locations, representing a massive, highly specific asset base that would take decades and tens of billions of dollars for a competitor to replicate.
- **Evidence:**
    - Capital expenditures for 2025 are expected to be between $3.0 and $3.2 billion, with the majority for new restaurant expansion. (Form 10-Q, Aug 06, 2025).
    - As of December 31, 2024, the company owned approximately 56% of the land and 80% of the buildings for restaurants in its consolidated markets. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
    - Net property and equipment on the balance sheet totaled $27.0 billion as of June 30, 2025. (Form 10-Q, Aug 06, 2025).
- **Score:** 3 / 5

**10) Customer Contracts & Revenue Visibility**
- **Reasoning:** The company has minimal direct, long-term contracts with its end customers, leading to low revenue visibility on a per-customer basis. However, its revenue from franchisees, based on a percentage of sales and minimum rent payments from 20-year contracts, provides a highly stable and predictable cash flow stream for the corporation. The score is low because the moat dimension is defined by *customer* contracts, not franchisee contracts.
- **Evidence:**
    - Revenues from franchised restaurants are primarily royalties based on a percentage of sales and rent, stipulated in franchise agreements that generally have 20-year terms. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
    - For the six months ended June 30, 2025, revenues from franchised restaurants were $7.874 billion, a highly stable and recurring source of income. (Form 10-Q, Aug 06, 2025).
- **Score:** 1 / 5

**Moat Duration Justification:**
The primary sources of McDonald's moat—its globally recognized brand and its immense, efficient-scale distribution network—are exceptionally durable and show no signs of near-term erosion. Franchise agreements, which are typically 20 years in length, provide a long-term, stable structure that underpins the company's cost advantages and operational consistency. Given the enduring nature of its brand and physical footprint, the moat is estimated to be durable for at least 15-20 years.

**Top 3 Moat Erosion Scenarios:**
1.  **Significant Brand Damage:** A large-scale, poorly handled food safety or public health crisis could cause lasting damage to consumer trust in the brand, eroding its primary intangible asset. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
2.  **Franchisee System Disruption:** A major breakdown in the relationship between the company and its franchisees could disrupt operational consistency, slow investment in restaurant modernization, and harm the unit economics that fuel the company's cost advantages. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
3.  **Digital Disruption by a Competitor:** A competitor could leapfrog McDonald's in digital innovation, creating a superior customer experience through a more effective loyalty program, delivery integration, or personalization that shifts consumer preferences and market share. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).

**KPIs to Monitor:**
1.  **Global Comparable Sales Growth:** Continued positive growth, particularly in guest counts, indicates brand relevance and pricing power.
2.  **Operating Margin %:** Should remain in the mid-to-high 40% range as guided; a sustained decline could signal erosion of cost advantages. (Form 10-Q, Aug 06, 2025).
3.  **Systemwide Sales to Loyalty Members:** Growth towards the $45 billion target indicates the strengthening of the digital network effect. (Form 10-K, Feb 25, 2025).
4.  **Franchisee Cash Flow:** A decline in franchisee profitability could signal stress in the system, a leading indicator of future relationship and reinvestment problems.
5.  **Net Restaurant Unit Growth:** Should align with company targets (approaching 1,800 net additions in 2025); a slowdown could indicate weakening distribution scale advantage. (Form 10-Q, Aug 06, 2025).
6.  **Brand Trust/Value Surveys:** Monitor third-party rankings of brand value and consumer trust for signs of decay in the core intangible asset.

The 4 / 5 moat rating is justified by McDonald's dominant and durable competitive advantages in intangible assets (brand) and efficient scale (distribution network), which receive the highest possible scores.
